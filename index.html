02 · The Idea

The iron in the letter

The mark is a single letter G, built with compass geometry but finished with soft, humanist terminals — drawn, not stamped out by a machine.

Inside it sits a structural cross: a horizontal beam — the letter's own bar — meeting a vertical support, locked at the center by a single rivet. That rivet is the load-bearing point. It is where the name lives.

铁 — iron, steel.
靖 — steady, secure, at peace.

Iron that holds steady under load. A founder's mark that can grow, in time, into a family seal. No year is engraved — the seal is meant to outlast any single date.

03 · Color

Graphite frame, copper light

05 · Lockups

Mark with name

The mark stands alone for icons and seals. When paired with the name, hold the spacing fixed: clear space equal to the rivet diameter on all sides. Name set in the display serif; identity line in mono.

06 · Usage

Hold the line

Do
  • Keep clear space ≥ one rivet diameter around the mark.
  • Use graphite on paper, paper on graphite — maximum contrast.
  • Reserve copper for pressed, foiled, or fine printed pieces.
  • Use the square chop for engineering and technical documents.
  • Default to the favicon form below ~48px.
Don't
  • Don't recolor the mark outside the palette.
  • Don't add a year or date to the seal.
  • Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the mark.
  • Don't place the full seal below 64px — it fills in.
  • Don't pair with any non-Latin type in the system.
